Manifold

Introduction: A manifold is a crucial component in various industrial systems, including oil and gas, chemical processing, and hydraulic systems. It serves as a junction point where multiple pipes or lines converge or diverge, allowing for the control and distribution of fluids or gases.

Key Functions: Manifolds perform several essential functions:

  • Distribution: Dividing a single fluid stream into multiple streams.
  • Collection: Combining multiple fluid streams into a single stream.
  • Control: Regulating flow rates and pressures using valves and other devices.
  • Isolation: Shutting off specific lines for maintenance or safety.

Types of Manifolds: Manifolds come in various designs to suit different applications:

  • Production Manifolds: Used in oil and gas to control the flow from multiple wells.
  • Hydraulic Manifolds: Used in hydraulic systems to distribute fluid to actuators.
  • Chemical Manifolds: Used in chemical processing to mix and distribute chemicals.
